Immigration Visa

According to a survey conducted by INTO University Partnerships, “76% of Indian students aspire to study abroad”.

A person is said to have immigrated when they move from one location to another in search of employment or better chances. The purpose of migration can be temporary or permanent. People who choose to live there permanently are referred to as "permanent residents" of that nation. A permanent resident is different from a citizen and holds the passport of another country. A person who lives outside of the nation in which they were born is referred to as an international immigrant. An immigrant is generally motivated by the desire to find new chances and a better way of life when they cross borders in pursuit of employment, education, and new perspectives.

Immigration Visa Eligibility

  • Basic eligibility (such as for the Express Entry of Canada, or SkillSelect of Australia)
  • Language proficiency
  • Health requirements to be confirmed by a medical examination by a panel doctor
  • A police clearance certificate (PCC)
  • Proof of funds (if necessary)

Requirements-

Each immigration programme has different eligibility requirements. The paperwork required to support the claims made in your application will vary depending on the programme to which you are applying. It is best to double-check your eligibility before applying. Ordinarily, the government concerned will verify your education, identity, work experience, and general background.

Steps to apply

  • STEP 1: Check eligibility.
  • STEP 2: Finding out if you score the required points, if applicable (67 points needed for Express Entry, 65 points for SkillSelect).
  • STEP 3: Arrange the list requirements with the help of Global Pathway overseas professionals.
  • STEP 4: Register an Expression of Interest profile
  • STEP 5: Receive an Invitation to Apply (ITA)
  • STEP 6: Submit completed application.

PERMANENT RESIDENCE VISA

Why settle:

Moving abroad is becoming more and more popular and has plenty of benefits such as travelling to new places, meeting new people and cultures. For some people, moving abroad is a necessity, while for others it’s a choice. As said earlier, moving abroad is more than just a fashion. It's about a willingness to relocate abroad for work, relationships or even a new adventure.

Government statistics show that every year, about 1 lakh people obtain citizenship in nations other than India. Nityanand Rai, the nation's Minister of State for Home, recently provided some disturbing statistics on Indians obtaining citizenship abroad in response to a question in the Lok Sabha. In 2016, more than the 1,41,000 people who acquired foreign citizenship in 2015 did so. This number kept rising till the year 2019. Due to Corona, this number decreased significantly in 2020 to somewhere around 88,000, but starting in 2021, it increased once more to close to a million. In other words, every day, more than 350 Indians obtain citizenship abroad.

Documentation needed:

Following are the documents that are required for applying a permanent visa:

  • Transcript (provisional or degree certificate, consolidated mark memo, individual marks sheet)
  • Passport
  • Birth certificate
  • IELTS (International English Language Testing System)
  • A police clearance certificate (PCC)
  • Funds (if necessary)
  • Digital photo copy
  • Federal PR & RPRF (Right of Permanent Residence Fee) Fee Provincial Application
  • Educational documents (starting from schooling up till your graduation or the highest degree of education)
  • Professional documents (OfferorAppointment orIncrement orTransfer letter, 3 months latest pay slip letter & IT Returns, Referral letter)

Processing:

Candidates should meet the Minimum eligibility criteria to apply for PR Visa.
Once the applicant meets the eligibility, it is taken into process, Starting with the Education evaluation, by having the applicant get done their English language exam. Next comes the Express Entry Profile, followed by PNP (Province Nomination Programs) applications. The client received the ITA( Invitation To Apply) Will complete the final application by uploading the required documents. And will submit the application by paying the application fee. Time taken is up to 6-8 months.
